\textbf{Estimating Band Means and Dust}

Let $m_{ib}$ be the mean magnitude of light curve $i$ in band $b$. We model
\begin{equation*}
m_{ib} = \beta_b + \alpha_i + d_i\delta_b + \sigma_{ib}
\end{equation*}
where $\beta_b$ is the mean offset for band $b$, $\alpha_i$ is the mean brightness of light curve $i$, $d_i$ is the amount of dust, $\delta_b$ is the extinction due to dust in band $b$, and $\sigma_{ib} \sim N(0,\sigma^2)$ independent. For identifiability we require $\sum \beta_b = 0$ (sum contrasts?). We can rewrite this model as
\begin{equation*}
  m_i \sim N(\beta + \alpha_i\V{1} + d_i\V{\delta},\sigma^2\M{I})
\end{equation*}


\begin{equation*}
  m_i \sim N(\beta + \alpha_i\V{1},\sigma^2\M{I} + d\V{\delta}\V{\delta}^T)
\end{equation*}
